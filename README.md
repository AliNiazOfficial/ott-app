## ABOUT TOOL :

OTT stands for "Over-The-Top" .

An OTT app is a streaming application that delivers video, audio, and other media content directly to viewers over the internet, bypassing traditional cable, broadcast, or satellite television platforms.

Instead of flipping through channels on a cable box, you use an app on a device connected to the internet to watch what you want, when you want.

Here is a breakdown of how they work, popular examples, and the different types available.

Key Characteristics

· Internet Delivery: Content is streamed via an internet connection (Wi-Fi or mobile data), not through a coaxial cable or satellite dish.
· Bypasses Traditional Gatekeepers: It goes "over" the top of cable TV providers.
· Device Agnostic: You can watch on Smart TVs, smartphones (iOS/Android), tablets, streaming sticks (Roku, Amazon Fire Stick), and game consoles.

Common Types of OTT Apps

1. Subscription Video on Demand (SVOD)

· How it works: Users pay a monthly or yearly fee for unlimited access to a content library.
· Examples: Netflix, Disney+, HBO Max, Hulu (ad-free plan), Amazon Prime Video.

2. Advertising-Based Video on Demand (AVOD)

· How it works: Content is free to watch, but you have to watch commercial advertisements.
· Examples: YouTube, Pluto TV, Tubi, Peacock (free tier).

3. Transactional Video on Demand (TVOD)

· How it works: Users pay a one-time fee to rent or buy a specific movie or TV episode. You don't own a library, just that specific title.
· Examples: Apple iTunes, Amazon Prime Video (rent/buy section), Google Play Movies & TV.

4. Hybrid Models

· How it works: Combines a subscription with ads (cheaper monthly fee but with commercials) or offers a basic free tier with ads and a premium paid tier without ads.
· Examples: Hulu, Peacock, Paramount+.

OTT vs. Traditional TV (Linear TV)

· Scheduling: OTT is on-demand (watch anytime); Traditional TV is linear (shows air at a specific time).
· Content: OTT often includes original programming (Netflix Originals) and deep libraries; Traditional TV relies on live events (news, sports) and scheduled programming.
· Contract: OTT is usually month-to-month with no long-term contracts; Traditional TV often requires 1-2 year contracts.
